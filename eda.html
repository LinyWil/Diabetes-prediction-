Diabetes Prediction -Feature Importance

  • According to the World Health Organisation (WHO), diabetes is considered a chronic disease and a leading cause of death worldwide.
  • Random Forest Classifier was used for model prediction as it showed high accuracy and good recall scores (measures completeness of positive predictions).
  • The model used the SMOTE technique to handle the imbalanced data. A few suggestions include the need to consider other techniques like oversampling or undersampling, for more efficiency in treating the imbalanced data.

  • Feature importance depicts the importance of features such as HbA1c level(0.43) and blood glucose level (0.25) as a good predictor of identifying diabetes patients.
  • Research studies have proven that elevated levels of HbA1c show a risk for cardiovascular disease and stroke in people with diabetes.

  • Next age (0.18) was the third important feature, as type 2 diabetes is more commonly found in the older age group.
  • Body Mass Index (BMI) was the next highlighted feature, as elevated levels of BMI are risk factors for type 2 diabetes. The findings align with research studies conducted in this field
  • Factors such as smoking history, hypertension, and heart disease showed less impact in this study.
